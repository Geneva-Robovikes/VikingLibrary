Killough Drive

What is it?

Killough drive is a method of driving using omni wheels with rollers. Three omni wheels in a triangular arrangement allow the robot to drive in any direction without changing the orientation of the robot. A robot with a conventional drivetrain (four or six wheels) must turn in the direction it needs to drive. A killough robot can move in any direction without first turning and is also called a holonomic drive.

Properties

Left Motor
The motor to use as the left motor for robot drive actions.
Right Motor
The motor to use as the right motor for robot drive actions.
Back Motor
The motor to use as the back motor for robot drive actions.
Maximum Output
Maximum allowable output to the motors, generally this should be one.
Safety Enabled
Whether or not motor safety should be in effect
Safety Expiration Time
How long before motor safety kicks in.
